# DeepSearch Prompts

Use these as working prompts for the three public research agents. Replace placeholders with the user's project data. Output in French by default.

## 1. Market Awareness

Research the market awareness level for `{offer}` in `{market}` for `{target_avatar}`.

Cover:
- Current awareness stage: unaware, problem aware, solution aware, product aware, most aware.
- Market size signals and practical reachable audience.
- Search, social, community, review, and media signals.
- Buying triggers, urgency, objections, and common language.
- What the market already believes about similar solutions.
- What this implies for Meta Ads hooks, offers, proof, and landing angle.

End with:
- Awareness verdict.
- 5 usable ad angles.
- 5 claims that need proof before being used.

## 2. Competitor Landscape

Research public competitors for `{offer}` in `{market}`.

For each relevant competitor:
- Target audience.
- Core offer and pricing if public.
- Acquisition funnel: Meta Ads, landing page, lead magnet, webinar, call booking, checkout, etc.
- Messaging angles and repeated hooks.
- Visual style and format patterns.
- Public proof signals: testimonials, reviews, case studies, social proof.
- Customer likes/dislikes from public reviews or comments.

End with:
- Saturated angles.
- White spaces.
- Differentiation opportunities.
- Competitors to prioritize in Meta Ads Library.

## 3. Psychographic Research

Research `{target_avatar}` for `{offer}` in `{market}`.

Cover:
- Daily pains and frustrations in the buyer's own words.
- Desired outcomes and status aspirations.
- Buying fears and objections.
- Failed alternatives they have tried.
- Beliefs about why the problem exists.
- Moments that trigger action.
- Language patterns from public forums, comments, reviews, social posts, and search snippets.

End with:
- Core emotional tension.
- 10 verbatim-style phrases suitable for ethical ad inspiration.
- 5 objections and how the strategy should answer them.
- Creative brief implications.

## Source Rules

- Use public sources only.
- Cite URL and access date for factual claims.
- Mark uncertain claims as hypotheses.
- Do not cite private LeadFactory files or client examples.
